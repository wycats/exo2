The design principles define how Exosuit should feel across documentation and
product-adjacent surfaces: local, inspectable, dense, native, and built around
developer judgment.

    <div class="exo-principles-intro">
      <span class="exo-number">02</span>
      <h2>Design Principles</h2>
      <p>
        Exosuit should feel like a collaborative cockpit: a compact, high-trust
        surface that helps humans and agents stay oriented, make decisions, and
        verify progress.
      </p>
    </div>

    <div class="exo-principles-layout">
      <section aria-label="Design principles">
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon caret"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number">01</span>
            <h3>Augmenting, not autonomous</h3>
            <p>
              Exosuit strengthens developer judgment with context, steering,
              and verification; it does not replace the human decision-maker.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon chat"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number green">02</span>
            <h3>Cockpit, not chatbot</h3>
            <p>
              Orientation, signals, controls, and workflow state matter more
              than a generic conversational shell.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon lines"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number yellow">03</span>
            <h3>Dense, not cramped</h3>
            <p>
              Compact information is good, but hierarchy and scannability must
              stay intact.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon search"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number">04</span>
            <h3>Local and inspectable</h3>
            <p>
              State should feel grounded in the workspace, visible to the user,
              and understandable at every step.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon code"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number green">05</span>
            <h3>Native, not over-branded</h3>
            <p>
              Inside product surfaces, Exosuit should feel at home in developer
              tools rather than like a flashy SaaS layer.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
        <div class="exo-principle-row">
          <div class="exo-icon-tile"><span class="exo-icon shield"></span></div>
          <div>
            <span class="exo-number yellow">06</span>
            <h3>Tactical, not militarized</h3>
            <p>
              Use the language of steering, phases, rails, and signals, while
              avoiding combat imagery or aggressive futurism.
            </p>
          </div>
        </div>
      </section>
